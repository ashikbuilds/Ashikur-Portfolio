About

Making models explain themselves

I'm a Computer Science graduate and current Research Assistant at Al Hikma Research Lab, working across machine learning, deep learning, natural language processing and computer vision. My research centers on building AI that is accurate and interpretable — from multilingual hate‑speech detection to healthcare diagnostics — and I'm currently exploring graph neural networks and transformer‑based architectures for clinical and safety‑critical problems.

  • Explainable AI

    SHAP, LIME, Grad‑CAM and permutation importance, used to keep high‑performing models auditable.

  • Multilingual NLP

    Transformer ensembles — BERT, RoBERTa, DeBERTa — for cross‑lingual hate‑speech detection.

  • Graph Neural Networks

    Patient‑similarity modeling and graph‑based classification for multi‑stage disease diagnosis.

  • Healthcare AI

    Diabetes, hepatitis C, diabetic retinopathy and accident‑severity prediction from real clinical data.
